# Prevention of Sexual Harassment (PoSH)

## 1. What kinds of behaviour cause sexual harassment?

Sexual harassment means any unwanted behaviour of a sexual nature that makes someone feel uncomfortable, unsafe, or disrespected.

Some examples are:

- Making dirty jokes or sexual comments  
- Commenting on someone’s body or clothes  
- Sending inappropriate messages, photos, or videos  
- Unwanted touching, hugging, or standing too close  
- Staring at someone in a way that makes them uncomfortable  
- Asking for sexual favours for promotion, good marks, or benefits  
- Repeatedly asking someone out after they said no  
- Spreading rumours about someone’s personal life  

## 2. What would you do if you face or see such behaviour?

If I face sexual harassment:

- I will tell the person to stop.  
- I will save messages or proof.  
- I will note down what happened.  
- I will report it to HR or the Internal Complaints Committee.  
- I will not ignore repeated behaviour.

If I see someone being harassed:

- I will support the person.  
- I will not blame them.  
- I will encourage them to report it.  
- If needed, I will report it myself.
