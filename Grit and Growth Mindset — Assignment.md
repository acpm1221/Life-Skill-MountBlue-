

## 1. Grit — Overview

Grit is the ability to stay committed to long-term goals with strong determination and steady effort. It is not about short bursts of motivation, but about continuing to work hard even when things become difficult, boring, or slow. People with grit do not give up easily; they stay focused and keep moving forward despite obstacles.

---

## 2. Understanding Growth Mindset — Overview

A growth mindset is the idea that intelligence, talent, and skills are not fixed. Instead, they can be developed through practice, learning, and persistence. Individuals with a growth mindset see challenges as opportunities to improve. They treat mistakes as learning experiences and believe effort leads to progress.

---

## 3. Internal Locus of Control

### Meaning of Internal Locus of Control

Internal locus of control means believing that your results in life are mostly influenced by your own choices, actions, and efforts. Instead of blaming luck, environment, or other people, you take responsibility for your success and failures.

### Important Idea

To stay motivated and productive, focus on things you can control—your discipline, learning habits, time management, and attitude. When you accept responsibility, you gain more confidence and direction in your life.

---

## 4. Important Practices for Developing a Growth Mindset

- Understand that improvement comes with practice  
- Accept challenges instead of avoiding them  
- Treat mistakes as lessons  
- Stay determined after setbacks  
- Value hard work more than instant results  
- Keep upgrading your knowledge  
- Replace negative self-talk with positive learning statements  
- Welcome constructive feedback  
- Try new and uncomfortable tasks  
- Practice consistently
