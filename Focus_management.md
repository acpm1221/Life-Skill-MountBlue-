# Focus Management 

## Question 1: What is Deep Work?

Deep work is when you focus **completely** on one hard task without any distractions.

No phone. No checking email. No switching tabs. Just you and the work. Cal Newport says this type of focus is rare today, but it helps you create better stuff faster.

## Question 2: How to do deep work properly?

1.  Don't try to focus all day. Do deep work for 60-90 minutes, then take a real break.
2.  Put your phone in another room. Close all tabs. Tell people not to disturb you.
3.  Do deep work at the same time everyday. Your brain gets used to it.
4.  Don't check email or messages while doing deep work. Every time you switch, you lose focus.

## Question 3: How can I use this in my daily life?

*   Find 1 hour in your morning when nobody needs you. Use that for your hardest task.
*   Keep phone in bag or another room. Out of sight helps a lot.
*   If 1 hour feels too long, start with 25 minutes. Then increase slowly.
*   After deep work, do something mindless. Walk. Wash dishes. Let brain rest.

## Question 4: What are the dangers of social media?

*   Apps are made to grab attention. Using them makes it harder to focus on work.
*   Seeing everyone's happy moments makes you feel your life is boring.
*   You open phone for 2 minutes, end up scrolling 40 minutes.
*   You forget how to just sit and think. You always need phone stimulation.
