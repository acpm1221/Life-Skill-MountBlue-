# Listening and Communication

## 1. Being a Good Listener

Being a good listener means giving full attention to the person who is speaking.

- Focus on the speaker  
- Avoid distractions  
- Do not interrupt  
- Use simple words like **"okay"** or **"I understand"**  
- Nod your head to show interest  
- Repeat the main idea in your own words  

---

## 2. Reflective Listening

Reflective listening means showing the speaker that you understand them.

- Say back what the speaker said in simple words  
- Notice feelings as well as words  
- Ask small questions to confirm understanding  
- Help the speaker feel heard and respected  

---

## 3. Problems in Listening

Listening becomes difficult because of:

- Thinking about your reply too early  
- Noise or other distractions  
- Assuming you already know the message  
- Strong emotions like anger or stress  

---

## 4. Improving Listening Skills

You can improve listening by:

- Paying full attention  
- Keeping your phone away  
- Using short responses like **"I see"** or **"Go on"**  
- Using positive body language  
- Staying calm and patient  

---

## 5. Passive Communication

Passive communication means not saying what you really feel.

This happens when:

- You want to avoid arguments  
- You feel scared or unsure  
- You put others first all the time  
- You stay quiet even when you disagree  

---

## 6. Aggressive Communication

Aggressive communication means speaking in a harsh way.

This happens when:

- You feel very angry  
- You want control  
- You ignore othersâ€™ feelings  
- You speak loudly or rudely  

---

## 7. Passive-Aggressive Communication

Passive-aggressive communication is indirect.

It happens when:

- You feel angry but do not say it clearly  
- You use sarcasm  
- You give silent treatment  
- You keep anger inside  

---

## 8. Assertive Communication

Assertive communication is clear and respectful.

You can practice it by:

- Clearly saying how you feel  
- Respecting yourself and others  
- Speaking calmly and confidently  
- Using positive body language  
- Solving problems early
